# GTM — CredLens Go-To-Market Strategy

This document details the targeting parameters, marketing channels, and customer acquisition playbook for **CredLens** to drive high-intent leads to Credex.

---

## 🎯 Target User Profile (ICP)

CredLens is not built for "generic startups". Our Ideal Customer Profile is:
- **Role**: Technical Founder, CTO, or Lead Platform Engineer.
- **Company Stage**: Seed to Series A startup.
- **Team Size**: 4 to 25 engineers.
- **Characteristics**: The team has adopted generative AI tools (Cursor, Claude, Copilot, ChatGPT) dynamically over the last 12 months. They lack a dedicated software procurement manager, and the engineering lead is paying for AI tools via their own credit card, expensing it monthly without monitoring overlap or seat minimum waste.

Right before a user lands on CredLens, they are experiencing mild operational friction or running month-end financial reviews. They are searching or thinking about:

## 🚀 Playbook to Acquire First 100 Users in 30 Days (With $0 Budget)

We will execute three organic distribution plays:

### Play 1: The "Hacker News & Show HN" Launch (Days 1–5)
We will submit CredLens to Hacker News with an educational, builder-focused title:
*Title*: *Show HN: An open-source spend auditor for startup AI stacks*
We will write a transparent launch comment outlining:
- The actual waste rules we discovered (such as Claude's 5-seat Team standard minimum overhead).
- A breakdown of verified 2026 pricing.
- An open invitation for builders to critique our logic. This triggers discussions and drives immediate traffic.

### Play 2: Twitter/X Micro-Auditing DMs (Days 6–20)
We will use X search to find founders complaining about their monthly AI bills or comparing tools:
- Search queries: `"my claud/cursor bill"`, `"copilot cursor overlap"`.
- We will send a polite, personalized DM: *"Hey, noticed you were looking at Cursor vs Copilot billing. Built a quick interactive auditor that maps out licensing redundancies and flags seat count waste. Free, no-login required: [URL]"*.

### Play 3: Tech Lead Cold Outreach via expensed receipt reports (Days 21–30)
We will scrape public tech startup listings on LinkedIn for companies with 5–15 employees and cold email the CTO:
*Subject*: *Quick check on Cursor / Claude licensing overlap at [Company]*
*Body*: *"Hi [Name], notice your team is scaling development. Most startups your size overspend by $100–$300/mo by paying duplicate Copilot/Cursor licenses or hitting seat minimums. Created a quick 60-second calculator to scan your stack. Open-source, no login required: [URL]"*.
